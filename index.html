The world is silent.

Everyone is asleep.

But my mind keeps thinking.

The silence isn't empty. It's heavy, pressed down by the weight of a thousand 'what ifs.' The clock on the nightstand glows like an accusation. The mind doesn't rest; it wakes up.

In here, thoughts aren't memories. They're predators. One begets two, two begets a hundred, all echoing off the walls of the maze, until you can't tell which thought was yours to begin with.

The maze hasn't vanished. But now, bathed in the light of a new day, its walls don't look like walls at all. They look like paths. And you have all the time in the world to choose which one to take.
